Our 5-Step Eco-Friendly Process

Every Triple G junk removal project follows our meticulous process to maximize recycling and donation while minimizing landfill impact.

1

On-Site Sorting

Our trained team carefully sorts all items directly at your location into three categories:

  • Recyclables: Metals, plastics, glass, paper, and electronics
  • Donatables: Gently used furniture, appliances, clothing, and household items
  • Landfill-bound: Only items that cannot be recycled or donated

This initial sorting ensures maximum diversion from landfills from the very beginning.

2

Separate Transport

We load sorted items into separate containers on our specialized trucks:

  • Designated compartments for different material types
  • Protective wrapping for delicate donatable items
  • Secure transportation to prevent contamination between categories
  • Direct routing to appropriate recycling centers and donation partners

This separation preserves item quality and prevents recyclable contamination.

3

Local Recycling Partners

We work directly with Redlands-area recycling facilities to process materials:

  • Redlands Recycling Center for paper, cardboard, and plastics
  • Inland Empire Metal Recycling for all ferrous and non-ferrous metals
  • E-Waste Collective for electronics and computer components
  • Green Wood Recycling for lumber and yard waste processing

These partnerships ensure materials are properly processed according to California regulations.

4

Community Donations

Gently used items are donated to local Redlands charities and organizations:

  • Family Service Association of Redlands - Furniture and household goods
  • Redlands Salvation Army - Clothing and small appliances
  • Goodwill of Southern California - Various reusable items
  • Redlands Homeless Shelter - Essential items for those in need
  • Habitat for Humanity ReStore - Building materials and furniture

We provide donation receipts for tax deduction purposes upon request.

5

Transparency & Reporting

We provide detailed documentation of our environmental impact:

  • Weight tickets from recycling facilities
  • Donation receipts from charity partners
  • Landfill diversion percentage for your specific project
  • Environmental impact report showing CO2 savings

This transparency builds trust and shows exactly how your junk removal made a difference.

Our Local Redlands Partners

Building a greener community through strategic partnerships with local recycling facilities and charitable organizations.

Redlands Recycling Center

Our primary partner for paper, cardboard, glass, and plastic recycling. Certified by the California Department of Resources Recycling and Recovery (CalRecycle).

Inland Empire Metal Recycling

Specializes in processing ferrous and non-ferrous metals. All metals are sorted and prepared for reuse in manufacturing, keeping them out of landfills.

E-Waste Collective

Certified electronic waste processor that safely disassembles electronics, recovers valuable materials, and properly disposes of hazardous components.

Family Service Association of Redlands

Local charity providing furniture and household goods to families in need throughout the Redlands community. Our primary donation partner since 2018.

Redlands Salvation Army

Accepts clothing, small appliances, and household items. Proceeds from sales support their adult rehabilitation programs in the Inland Empire.

Habitat for Humanity ReStore

Accepts building materials, furniture, and appliances. Sales support Habitat's mission to build affordable housing in San Bernardino County.
